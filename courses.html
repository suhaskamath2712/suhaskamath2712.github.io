Academic Course Reviews

A compilation of courses taken during my M. Tech at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, including reviews, ratings, grades, and code repositories.

E0261

Database Management Systems

Semester 1 Grade: 9 (Highest Awarded)
Review: 10/10

This was one of the best courses I have ever taken at IISc, second only to "Topics in Database Management Systems". This course gives a good overview of database management systems. It covers topics such as query optimisation, transaction management, concurrency control, and recovery. It also covers topics such as distributed databases, data warehousing and data mining.

Prof. Jayant's humourous anecdotes keep the class active and engaged, and students genuinely look forward to the classes. This course is nothing but an epitome of how teaching must be done at the graduate school level.

The course project is a beautiful addition to the course; which complements the subjects learnt in the class nicely. It also gives students the chance to interact with industry professionals, making the course feel special.

★★★★★

DS221

Introduction to Scalable Systems

Semester 1 Grade: 9
Review: 8/10

This is the best hardcore course offered by the CDS department. The assignments for this course are very well designed and cover a wide range of topics. This course gives a good overview of scalable systems. It covers topics such as data structures, algorithms, basic complexity theory and parallel computing.

Prof. Satish's classes may seem a bit boring, but they are extremely packed with knowledge. Prof. Chirag's classes, on the other hand, are more interactive and active. The assignments cover the theoretical knowledge learnt in class in a proper fashion.

DS289

Numerical Linear Algebra

Semester 1 Grade: 7
Review: 1/10

This course covers the fundamentals of numerical linear algebra in the first third of the semester. After that, the course delves into advanced topics which are honestly not useful to students who aren't willing to join the instructor's lab. This makes the latter two-thirds of the course feel like too much to bear, considering the course is only three credits.

Furthermore, the exams are kept completely disjoint from the course content making the exams feel like pure torture. This is not just my opinion; this comes from a class whose average was 9.5/35.

The TAs are even more obnoxious than the instructor. The TAs frequently lie about score statistics to students, such as inflating the average grade. This makes a huge difference, especially when the course is relatively graded. The TAs also seem to have ego issues, and give additional marks to those that "suck up" to them.

This is, by far, the worst course I have taken at IISc.

E0361

Topics in Database Systems

Semester 2 Grade: 9 (Highest Awarded)
Review: 10/10

This course is the successor to E0261 (Database Management Systems). This is, by far the best course I have ever taken at IISc. In this course, students have to read research papers and have to present them to the professor and their peers.

Because the instructor is open-minded, the presentations aren't kept formal; memes and humourous analogies are actively encouraged. After each presentation, the instructor sends out a list of research questions that the students need to answer. These questions sometimes cover aspects outside the research paper, which means that the students have to expand their range of thought. It is a really good practice.

★★★★★

DS256

Scalable Systems for Data Science

Semester 2 Grade: 9 (Highest Awarded)
Review: 8/10

This is an extremely practical course. It covers topics such as distributed systems, parallel computing, and big data processing. The assignments are very well designed and cover a wide range of topics. The classes are engaging and the instructor ensures that students have a good understanding of the topics covered.

However, the handling of the course could have been better. The course was supposed to have three assignments, but the last two were combined due to lack of compute. For the same reason, we were given only seven "slots" to run the second assignments. The assignments were fun to do, but were extremely stress-inducing.

DS207

Introduction to Natural Language Processing

Semester 2 Grade: 8
Review: 6/10

This course is a good introduction to the world of natural language processing. However, due to the sheer enrollment numbers for the course, the enjoyment factor takes a massive hit. The assignments seem to be designed by AI, which is never welcome in a course wherein the instructor has banned AI altogether.

Furthermore, the course has two instructors, who kept swapping in and out. Every other class feels like a new class, and there is no continuity at all, as both the instructors teach completely differently. This made the course content difficult and hard to follow. The course is also bloated with too much about machine learning and reinforcement learning, which is honestly not needed in an NLP course.

There were multiple guest lectures, which are always welcome. However, the instructor decided to include these guest lectures in the final exam, which is never a good idea.

★★★☆☆
